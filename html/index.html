About me

Hello! I am a sophomore at Brown University from [location]. Currently concentrating in computer science, I’m planning to follow the design+AI track due to my curiosities in human-computer interaction. Feel free to follow the timeline to unravel the journey that led me to my interests in accessible design, user experience, and socially responsible computing!

2019

Technology Communication

Before my exposure to computer science, I worked as a lab assistant for a chemistry laboratory, and found that I was extremely drawn to explaining complex terms to and presenting information to connect the public. This led me to found a science journal for high school students in my local town; this was the project kickstarted my interest in the field of ‘science/tech communication’. In my project link below, I present the content my team has created over the years! :)

2020

App Development!

Prior to Brown, I took a gap year where I worked on an environmental food surplus mobile application, which allowed users to 'rescue' food waste left over at the end of the day from restaurants. This was the experience that introduced me to the world of programming, but also the importance of socially responsible programming. Throughout the development process of this application, I began to recognize the immediate social implications of our design choices, such as the effects of inflexible report systems for our food delivery workers. From this, I decided to enroll in courses that would expose me to more of these issues, as well as its solutions.

2022

Undergraduate Teaching Assistant

I am now a Socially Responsible Computing Teaching Assistant for an introductory CS class at Brown. My responsibilities include designing the course curriculum to integrate social impact topics via weekly interactive labs, as well as extra-credit discussions. Additionally, I present lectures on ethical computing (misinformation, algorithmic justice, freedom of expression etc.) to ~400 students every week.
