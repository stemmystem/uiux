"Efficient NFT Interface"

Designing an app interface for an NFT Research Startup


Overview:

My team of 4 were tasked with designing an application interface for our chosen startup. Throughout this project, we went through multiple iterations of our figma design - the first was via team critique, the second via external group feedback in a studio setting, and the third was after extensive user testing on usertesting.com. Below you will find our design process and multiple drafts to reach our final UI design.

Startup Background:

NFTScoring is the place for you to discover, analyze and trade NFTs, giving you the superpowers to understand the NFT market in any given moment, make the best decisions, and take faster actions. We designed a mobile interface to help experienced NFT collectors more easily find investment data. Our interface helps individuals with significant NFT experience make better decisions

Iteration 2: Interactive prototype and feedback

Observations:


We built a figma mockup of the wireframes (shown below) to bring to the studio critique. Overall, feedback was mostly positive with the main suggestions being differentiate the buy and sell pages, add an "add favorites" function, give users more flexibility to edit their profile, and our overall dark mode was hard to read. With this feedback, we redesigned the interface with more attractive colors to drown out the black background. On top of this we added an option to favorite a particular NFT, and gave users a better experience when viewing their own profile.

Iteration 3: User Testing

User Testing

We asked users on UserTesting.com to complete a few tasks and give us feedback on our design. They were given the following introduction:

You are an avid collector of NFTs (essentially unique virtual trading cards) looking to buy and sell NFTs. Instead of a mobile app, you will be using an interactive mockup on prototyping software to simulate the experience of using your phone. When the prototype loads in, click where you would be tapping your finger on a phone to use the interface. Buy and sell an NFT using the platform, and try to think aloud while using the interface. You will not need to enter information to use the platform, simply press “login” once at the sign-in screen.

Users were then asked to complete the following tasks:

1. Favorite the Space Riders NFT collection
2. Buy an individual NFT from the Space Riders NFT collection.
3. View your own NFT collection


Overall, the prototype performed quite well in the user testing. The main issues with the prototype were with figma itself or the limited connections between screens. The users were highly successful in both favoriting the collection and buying an NFT. Two of the users struggled with finding the collection, though some of that issue came down to the figma. There were multiple suggestions to change the navigation bar at the bottom, and the existing testing issues were with finding the collection, so most of the improvements should be on that navbar. Tangibly, we could increase the font and icon sizes and move the collection tab into the profile section as suggested by users.
