"A Night Owl's Saviour"

An analysis of vending machine use on college campuses via the use of personas and storyboarding


Overview:

We were tasked with creating personas and storyboards to illustrate user journeys from extensive user and product research. Research included real-time sketching, noting down observations across ~20 users, and interviewing 3 users.

I chose vending machines, as it is a popular convenience choice for college students who need a quick energy boost, or a back-up option to traditional dining halls when they aren’t available. I have outlined my research methods below!

Step 2: User research

Observations:


1. All users:

- Had to swipe more than one time to get the machine to register their cash
- Used flex points rather than credit card or cash
- Seemed to struggle when they bent down to swipe their card
- Were confused as to when they could select a snack
- Opted for a high-sugar snack

2. Some users:

- Had a skittles packet stuck and had to shake the vending machine to dispense their snack
- Had to press the snack code buttons multiple times to get their desired item

Questions

1. What is your most common way of getting food?

2. Why did you use the vending machine, rather than [most common way of getting food]?

3. How do you benefit from your interaction with the vending machine?

4. What activity were you engaging in before coming here, and where are you going after this?

5. How far did you walk to get to this vending machine? (from last point of rest)

6. Was your experience with the vending machine what you expected?

Responses/Takeaways

- Most students mentioned cafeterias as their usual foo dsource

- Students tend to use vending machines when their first-choice options aren't available, like stores were closed, to financial reasons like using flex points

- Students mentioned caffeine as a convenient course of caffeine to help keep awake, or get through the day from lack of sleep (whether this is from an early mornign or late night)

- Students don't have to walk very far to use vending machines

Step 3: Personas

Based off observations and responses, two personas were developed for users of the vending machine at Blue Room.

1. Last-minute Larry...


is a sophomore at Brown who is studying at the Blue Room. He has a computer science project due at 9am the next day, and it is currently 3am. He goes to the vending machine in search of caffeine to wake himself up.

The instructions for the payment methods were too unclear for Larry, who is extremely fatigued, sleepy, and has brain fog, so he was confused by whether the machine acceped flex points or not. Larry represents a user of the chosen interface because most people that I interviewed was seeking an item to remedy a negative bodily symptom, and one common one was sleepiness.

2. Busy Bruce....


is a student-athlete who just finished his 6am lift workout for football training. It is currently 8:30am and he is extremely hungry but the lines at the ratty are too long, so he opts for food from the vending machine.

Bruce is in a hurry, so he has trouble with the number buttons on the interface, but when he finally gets a snack it gets stuck. Bruce represents a user of this interface because he opted for a vending machine due to long lines at the traditional dining hall and lack of time.

Step 4: Storyboarding


Last-minute Larry was chosen for storyboarding as he is the most common representation of someone that uses this interface, based on student observations.
