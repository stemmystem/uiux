"Connecting Young Scientists"

Founding a peer-review science journal for high schoolers in [location]!


Overview:

In my hometown, there were not many existing scientific journals targetted at middle-high schoolers. Thus, I decided to found a peer-review journal for aspiring scientsits and developed a website to host this platform. SCIENTIA encourages the whole community o understand the significance of science beyond the classroom, and to share their scientific curiosities. With its interdisciplinary approach, SCIENTIA is the junction between scientific logic and artful creativity. We accept submissions that relate to any of the 3 branches of the natural sciences (biology, chemistry, physics), or the formal sciences (computer science, etc.) whether it be in the form of a technical paper or a poem.

Click on the image below to visit the site!

Web principles:

We decided to design a user-friendly website that was accessible to scientists and non-scientists alike. To attract visitors, we transformed sophisticated article names into playful titles, or used engaging gifs to communicate complex topics. Our articles sparked conversation amongst the community, and before we knew it, we had expanded it to other schools in [location], allowing curious students to connect. Now, the website receives visits from over 10 countries.
